"What you can't measure, you can't improve." — And most HR teams are flying blind. We decided to change that.

Over the last quarter, we built a comprehensive Human Resources Analytics system in Power BI — tracking everything from headcount and compensation to performance ratings and attrition patterns. Here's what a $759M payroll looks like when you actually study it:

— financial snapshot

Compensation

Total payroll of $759M across 9K employees. Avg salary sits at $85K — competitive across all 7 departments.

Salary Equity

Salary distributed nearly equally by education level — Bachelor, PhD, Master & High School each at ~25% of total spend.

Attrition Cost

Finance & IT show highest attrition at 13%. Every % point costs us ~$7.6M in rehiring + training — a number that moves fast.

Overtime Load

30% of the workforce logs overtime. Finance & IT lead at 36% OT — a direct signal of under-resourcing we're addressing now.

"The real insight wasn't in any single chart. It was in seeing that our highest-performing department (Operations — 329 high performers) also carried our largest headcount AND the same attrition rate as Finance. Scale doesn't protect you from churn."

— 3 lessons for every people leader

1. Geographic concentration is a risk. 72% of our workforce sits in New York. That's a single point of failure — regulatory, talent market, and cost-wise. We're actively rebalancing.

2. Overtime is a lagging signal, not a badge of honor. 30% overtime means workload design is broken — not that your people are dedicated. Fix the system.

3. Education-salary equity matters. Equal salary distribution across Bachelor, Master, PhD, and High School graduates signals role-based compensation — not credential inflation. That's intentional and we're proud of it.
